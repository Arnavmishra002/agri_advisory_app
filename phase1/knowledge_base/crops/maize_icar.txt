ICAR Package of Practices — Maize (Zea mays)
Source: ICAR-Indian Institute of Maize Research (IIMR), Ludhiana
Season: Kharif (June–October); Rabi (November–March in South India)

VARIETIES
Hybrids (most productive):
DKC-9144: High yield, drought tolerant, popular in Karnataka, AP.
NK-6240: Disease resistant. Suitable for heavy rainfall zones.
Pioneer 30V92: Early maturing 95 days. Good for multiple cropping.
Composite varieties (open-pollinated, seed can be saved):
Vivek QPM-9 (Quality Protein Maize): High lysine+tryptophan. Better for human consumption and livestock.
African Tall: Fodder purpose.

SOWING
Kharif: June 15 – July 15 (sow with onset of monsoon).
Rabi (South India): October 15 – November 15.
Spacing: 60×25 cm (row×plant). For hybrids: 60×20 cm.
Seed rate: 20–25 kg/ha (hybrids); 18–20 kg/ha (composites).
Depth: 3–5 cm. Do not sow deeper — germination affected.

SOIL
Well-drained loam or sandy loam, pH 6.0–7.5.
Cannot tolerate waterlogging — ensure drainage channels in field.
Maize is the 3rd most important cereal in India.

FERTILIZER (MOST CRITICAL CROP FOR N RESPONSE)
General dose: N:P:K = 120:60:40 kg/ha (Kharif hybrids).
Basal: Full P + Full K + 30 kg N/ha.
Top dress 1: 45 kg N/ha at knee-height stage (V4–V5, 25–30 DAS).
Top dress 2: 45 kg N/ha at tasseling (50–55 DAS).
Zinc: 25 kg ZnSO4/ha if soil Zn < 0.5 ppm. Zinc deficiency very common in maize.
Symptoms of Zn deficiency: White to yellow stripes parallel to leaf midrib in young leaves.
Boron: 1 kg/ha if deficient (silk development affected).

IRRIGATION
Critical stages: Germination, Knee-height (V5), Tasseling/Silking (most critical — do NOT miss), Grain filling.
Total: 5–6 irrigations for full season.
Waterlogging for even 24 hours at silking causes 15–20% yield loss.

MAJOR PESTS

Fall Armyworm (Spodoptera frugiperda) — NEW INVASIVE PEST (arrived India 2018):
Most serious pest now.
Symptoms: Window pane damage on leaves; frass in whorl; C-shaped larvae.
Economic threshold: 5% plants showing fresh whorl damage.
Control: Spinetoram 11.7SC @ 0.5 mL/L (best); Emamectin benzoate 5SG @ 0.4g/L.
Early morning spray into whorl.
Pheromone traps: 10/ha for monitoring.
Biological: Trichogramma releases (1 lakh cards/ha) for egg parasitization.

Shoot Fly (Atherigona nubica):
Affects early-season seedlings.
Dead heart at 2–3 leaf stage.
Control: Carbofuran 3G @ 20 kg/ha in soil at sowing.

Stem Borer (Chilo partellus):
Damage similar to shoot fly but at later stages.
Control: Granules (Carbofuran 3G) in whorl at 15 and 30 DAS.

DISEASES

Turcicum Blight (Exserohilum turcicum):
Long elliptical, tan lesions with dark border on leaves.
Control: Mancozeb 75WP @ 2.5g/L at disease appearance.
Use resistant hybrids.

Downy Mildew (Peronosclerospora sorghi):
Systematic infection — stunted plants, white downy growth on leaves.
Control: Metalaxyl seed treatment @ 6g/kg seed.

Gibberella Stalk Rot:
Pink/red discoloration inside stalk near base.
Caused by excess N and poor drainage.
No curative spray — improve drainage and balanced nutrition.

HARVESTING
Harvest when grain moisture is 25–30% (fresh) or 15% (for storage).
Mechanical harvesting at 18–20% moisture.
Dry to 12–14% for storage — critical to prevent aflatoxin contamination.

YIELD
Kharif hybrids: 50–60 quintals/ha
Rabi hybrids: 60–70 quintals/ha (better conditions)
Composites: 35–45 quintals/ha

MSP 2024-25: Rs. 2,090 per quintal
