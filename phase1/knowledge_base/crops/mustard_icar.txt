ICAR Package of Practices — Mustard / Rapeseed (Brassica juncea / B. napus)
Source: ICAR-Directorate of Rapeseed-Mustard Research, Bharatpur
Season: Rabi (October–February)

VARIETIES
Pusa Bold: High erucic, bold seeds, 110 days. Most popular in North India.
RH-30: High yielding, early maturing 100 days. Suitable for late sowing.
Varuna (T-59): Classic variety, 125–130 days.
Pusa Agrani: Very early maturing 90–95 days for late sowing.
Pusa Mustard-30 (NPJ-112): Disease resistant.

SOWING
Optimal sowing: 1–15 October in North India; 15 Oct – 15 Nov in Central India.
Late sowing (after Nov 15): Use Pusa Agrani or RH-30.
Method: Line sowing is preferred — row spacing 30–45 cm, plant spacing 10–15 cm.
Seed rate: 5 kg/ha (lines); 4 kg/ha with dibbling.
Seed depth: 2–3 cm.

SOIL
Best soil: Well-drained sandy loam to loam, pH 6.0–7.5.
Avoid: Waterlogged, highly alkaline, or saline soils.
Irrigation: Mustard is moderately drought tolerant — requires much less water than wheat.

FERTILIZER
General dose: N:P:K = 60:40:30 kg/ha on normal soils.
Sulphur: CRITICAL for mustard — apply 30–40 kg/ha as gypsum or elemental S. Improves oil content and seed yield.
Apply all P, K, and S as basal. Split N: 50% basal + 50% at first irrigation (35–40 days).
Boron: 1 kg/ha if deficient. Improves pod set.

IRRIGATION
Critical stages: Rosette stage (35 DAS) and Pod formation stage (75–80 DAS).
Total: 2–3 irrigations for optimal yield.
NEVER irrigate at flowering — causes flower drop and reduces pod set.
Skip irrigation if rainfall ≥ 25mm within 7 days.

MAJOR PESTS

Aphids (Lipaphis erysimi):
Most damaging pest of mustard — can cause 30–80% yield loss.
Economic threshold: 40–50 aphids per plant.
Control: Dimethoate 30EC @ 1 L/ha OR Oxydemeton methyl 25EC @ 1 L/ha.
Spray in early morning to protect pollinators (bees critical for mustard pollination).
Biological: Conserve Coccinella beetles (natural predator).

Painted Bug (Bagrada hilaris):
Attack at seedling and pod stages.
Control: Malathion 50EC @ 1 L/ha.

Alternaria Blight (Alternaria brassicae):
Most common foliar disease.
Symptoms: Dark brown circular spots with yellow halo on leaves, stems, pods.
Control: Mancozeb 75WP @ 2g/L at 35–40 DAS and at pod formation.
Preventive seed treatment: Thiram @ 3g/kg.

White Rust (Albugo candida):
White pustules on undersides of leaves.
Control: Metalaxyl-Mancozeb (Ridomil Gold) @ 2.5g/L.

Sclerotinia Stem Rot:
Causes plant collapse.
Control: Carbendazim 50WP @ 1g/L at 50% flowering.

WEED MANAGEMENT
Isoproturon 75WP @ 1 kg/ha at 25–30 DAS (for narrow-leaf weeds).
Quizalofop ethyl 5EC @ 750 mL/ha for grass weeds at 3–4 leaf stage of weeds.
Hand weeding at 25 and 45 DAS if feasible.

HARVESTING
Harvest when 75–80% pods turn golden yellow and seeds turn brown.
Moisture at harvest: 12–15%.
Thresh within 24 hours of harvesting to avoid shattering losses.
Threshing by beating on jute mats or with thresher.

YIELD
Irrigated: 18–25 quintals/ha
Rainfed: 10–15 quintals/ha

MSP 2024-25: Rs. 5,650 per quintal

OIL CONTENT: 36–42% depending on variety and sulphur application.
