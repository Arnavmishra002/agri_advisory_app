ICAR Package of Practices — Soybean (Glycine max)
Source: ICAR-Indian Institute of Soybean Research (IISR), Indore
Season: Kharif (June–October). Major states: MP, Maharashtra, Rajasthan, Karnataka.

VARIETIES
JS-335: Most popular. 90–95 days. High yield, good seed quality.
JS-9305: Resistant to Yellow Mosaic Virus (YMV). 90 days.
NRC-37: Early maturing 85 days. Good for late sowing.
MACS-450: Suitable for Vidarbha and Marathwada.
Phule Kalyani: Maharashtra specific. Disease resistant.

SOWING
Optimal: June 20 – July 10 (wait for good monsoon). Sowing in dry soil = poor germination.
Spacing: 45×5 cm (row×plant) for short-duration; 45×10 cm for long-duration.
Seed rate: 70–80 kg/ha.
Seed treatment: Rhizobium inoculant + PSB @ 5g/kg seed. Saves 25–30 kg N/ha.
Also treat with Thiram 75WP @ 3g/kg for fungal diseases.
Inoculant and fungicide must be applied separately — never mix Thiram with Rhizobium.

SOIL
Black cotton soil or loamy soil, pH 6.0–7.5.
Good drainage essential — soybean is very sensitive to waterlogging.

FERTILIZER
Soybean fixes its own N through Rhizobium — only starter dose needed.
Dose: N:P:K = 20:80:40 kg/ha.
Full dose as basal.
Rhizobium inoculation can replace N fertilizer entirely if soil is inoculated.
Sulphur: 20 kg/ha as gypsum improves protein and oil content.
Micronutrients: Boron 1 kg/ha and Molybdenum 0.5 kg/ha if deficient.

IRRIGATION
Soybean is largely rainfed in India.
Critical stages needing irrigation: Flowering (R1), Pod filling (R5).
Light irrigation of 25–30 mm at these stages if rainfall gap > 15 days.

MAJOR PESTS

Girdle Beetle (Oberea brevis):
Most serious pest of soybean in India.
Damage: Female girdles stem, lays egg. Upper portion wilts and breaks.
Economic threshold: 2–5 eggs/girdled stems per plant.
Control: Chlorpyrifos 20EC @ 2.5 L/ha. Collect and destroy fallen plant parts.

Stem Fly (Melanagromyza sojae):
Damage: Maggots tunnel inside stem. Plant wilts suddenly at 3–4 weeks.
Control: Dimethoate 30EC @ 1 L/ha or Thiamethoxam 25WG @ 100g/ha at 15 and 30 DAS.

Tobacco Caterpillar (Spodoptera litura):
Defoliator — serious in August-September.
Control: Chlorpyrifos 20EC @ 2.5 L/ha OR Bt @ 2 kg/ha (early infestation only).
Pheromone traps: 10/ha.

Whitefly (Bemisia tabaci) — YMV vector:
Control whitefly to prevent Yellow Mosaic Virus.
Imidacloprid 70WG @ 35g/ha.

DISEASES

Yellow Mosaic Virus (YMV):
Most damaging disease. Transmitted by whitefly.
Symptoms: Bright yellow patches on leaves, stunted plant.
No cure. Remove infected plants immediately. Control whitefly.
Preventive: Use resistant varieties JS-9305.

Bacterial Pustule:
Brown-red pustules on underside of leaves.
Control: Copper oxychloride 50WP @ 3g/L.

Charcoal Rot / Stem Canker:
Late season. Affects yield.
Control: Proper drainage, crop rotation.

Pod Borer (Maruca vitrata):
Damages pods in August–September.
Control: Chlorpyrifos 20EC @ 2.5 L/ha at pod formation.

WEED MANAGEMENT
Imazethapyr 10SL @ 1 L/ha at 15–20 DAS (post-emergence).
Pendimethalin 30EC @ 3 L/ha pre-emergence (within 3 days of sowing).
One hand weeding at 30 DAS.

HARVESTING
Harvest when 80–90% pods turn brown-yellow.
Moisture: 12–15% for storage.
Avoid delay — pods shatter and cause losses.

YIELD
Rainfed: 15–25 quintals/ha
Irrigated: 25–35 quintals/ha

MSP 2024-25: Rs. 4,892 per quintal
Oil content: 18–22%. Protein content: 38–42%.
