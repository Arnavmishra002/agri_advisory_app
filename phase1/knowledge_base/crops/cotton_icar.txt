ICAR Package of Practices — Cotton (Gossypium hirsutum / G. arboreum)
Source: ICAR-Central Institute for Cotton Research (CICR), Nagpur
Season: Kharif (April–November in South; May–June in North)

VARIETIES AND HYBRIDS
Bt Hybrids (most common, >90% area): NHH-44, RCH-2, Bunny Bt, MRC-7017 Bt, Nath Seeds 6155.
Non-Bt varieties: MCU-5 (Tamil Nadu), LRK-516 (Rajasthan), Suraj (CG).
Important: Bt cotton controls American bollworm, pink bollworm — NOT sucking pests. Sucking pest management still required.
Refuge requirement: Plant 5% non-Bt seeds in refuge to delay resistance.

SOWING
Optimal: May 15 – June 30 (Kharif).
Spacing: 90×60 cm (irrigated); 60×30 cm (rainfed short-duration varieties).
Seed rate: 2.5–3 kg/ha (hybrid); 10–12 kg/ha (desi varieties).
Seed treatment: Imidacloprid 70WS @ 7g/kg seed (protects against sucking pests for 3–4 weeks).

SOIL
Best soil: Deep black cotton soil (Vertisol) with good water retention.
pH: 6.0–8.0. Tolerates slightly alkaline soils.
Drainage: Must be good — waterlogging causes severe root rot.

FERTILIZER
General dose: N:P:K = 120:60:60 kg/ha (irrigated hybrids).
Basal: Full P + Full K + 40 kg N/ha at sowing.
Top dress 1: 40 kg N/ha at 30–35 DAS (square formation).
Top dress 2: 40 kg N/ha at 60–65 DAS (boll development).
Foliar spray: 2% DAP (dissolved) at boll formation improves lint quality.
Boron: 0.2% borax spray at flowering to improve boll setting.

IRRIGATION (Irrigated crop)
Critical stages: Sowing (if dry), Square formation (40–45 DAS), Flowering (65–75 DAS), Boll development (85–100 DAS).
Avoid irrigation during peak flowering — causes boll shedding if done wrong.
Total: 6–8 irrigations for full season hybrid cotton.

MAJOR PESTS — SUCKING COMPLEX (most damaging group)

Whitefly (Bemisia tabaci):
Most serious pest — vector for Cotton Leaf Curl Virus (CLCuD).
Threshold: 6 whiteflies/leaf.
Control: Diafenthiuron 50WP @ 1g/L OR Pyriproxyfen 10EC @ 1 mL/L.
Avoid pyrethroids — cause resistance and BPH/mite flare-ups.

Thrips (Thrips tabaci):
Damage: Silvery appearance, leaf curling in early crop.
Control: Spinosad 45SC @ 0.3 mL/L at ETL of 10 thrips/leaf.

Jassid (Amrasca biguttula biguttula):
V-shaped yellowing from leaf tip.
Control: Dimethoate 30EC @ 2 mL/L OR Imidacloprid 17.8SL @ 0.3 mL/L.

Mealybug (Phenacoccus solenopsis):
White cottony masses on stems and leaves.
Control: Profenofos 50EC @ 2 mL/L. Remove infested plant parts first.

MAJOR PESTS — BOLLWORM COMPLEX

American Bollworm (Helicoverpa armigera):
Bt cotton provides ~90% control. Monitor for resistance.
Non-Bt control: Chlorpyrifos 20EC + Cypermethrin 10EC (combo) @ 2 L/ha.
Pheromone traps: 5/ha for monitoring.

Pink Bollworm (Pectinophora gossypiella):
Bt cotton effective. Check for internal boll damage.
Monitoring: Delta traps with PBW lure, 5/ha.

DISEASES

Cotton Leaf Curl Virus (CLCuD):
Vector: Whitefly. No curative treatment.
Prevention: Control whitefly, use tolerant varieties, remove infected plants.

Bacterial Blight (Xanthomonas campestris):
Angular water-soaked spots on leaves.
Control: Copper oxychloride 50WP @ 3g/L spray. Avoid overhead irrigation.

Fusarium/Verticillium Wilt:
Yellowing, wilting, death of plants.
Control: Trichoderma viride soil treatment @ 2.5 kg/ha. No effective foliar spray.

PICKING AND QUALITY
3–4 pickings per season. First picking most important.
Pick when bolls are fully open, dry mornings.
Do not mix lower grades.
Store in dry conditions — moisture causes mold.

YIELD
Irrigated Bt hybrid: 20–30 quintals (seed cotton) per ha = 8–12 quintals lint.
Rainfed: 12–18 quintals seed cotton/ha.

MSP 2024-25: Medium staple Rs. 6,620/q; Long staple Rs. 7,020/q.
