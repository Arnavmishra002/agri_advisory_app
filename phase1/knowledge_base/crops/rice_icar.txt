ICAR Package of Practices — Rice / Paddy (Oryza sativa)
Source: ICAR, Directorate of Rice Research, Hyderabad
Season: Kharif (June–November)

VARIETIES
Short-duration (105–115 days): MTU-1010, Swarna Sub1 (flood tolerant), DRR Dhan-44
Medium-duration (120–130 days): IR-64, Swarna, BPT-5204 (Samba Masuri)
Long-duration (140–150 days): Mahsuri, Lalat (Odisha)
Hybrid: DRRH-2, KRH-2 (yield 20–25% higher than HYV)

NURSERY PREPARATION
Nursery area: 1/20th of main field (500 sq.m per hectare).
Seed rate: 20–25 kg/ha for transplanting; 40 kg/ha for direct seeding.
Seed treatment: Trichoderma viride @ 4g/kg + soak in water 24h + incubate 48h.
Nursery period: 25–30 days (transplant at 4–5 leaf stage).

TRANSPLANTING
Spacing: 20×15 cm (plant × row) — gives 33 hills/sq.m.
Transplant 2–3 seedlings per hill.
Transplant within 2 days of nursery pulling to reduce transplanting shock.
Avoid transplanting during rain or strong sun.

SOIL AND WATER
Best soil: Clay or clay-loam, water retention capacity > 80%.
Puddling: 2–3 rounds of wet ploughing to destroy percolation.
Water depth: Maintain 5 cm standing water during vegetative stage.
Alternate Wetting and Drying (AWD): Allow soil to dry until cracks appear, then re-irrigate. Saves 25–30% water, same yield.
Drain field 7–10 days before harvest for mechanical harvesting.

FERTILIZER RECOMMENDATIONS
General dose: N:P:K = 120:60:60 kg/ha (transplanted) — adjust based on soil test.
Basal at transplanting: Full P + Full K + 30 kg N/ha.
Top dressing 1: 45 kg N/ha at active tillering (15–20 DAT).
Top dressing 2: 45 kg N/ha at panicle initiation (45–50 DAT).
Zinc: ZnSO4 @ 25 kg/ha if Zn deficient (common in alkaline soils).
Green manure: Sesbania aculeata (Dhaincha) incorporation 3 weeks before transplanting reduces N requirement by 30 kg/ha.

PEST MANAGEMENT

Stem Borer (Scirpophaga incertulas):
Symptoms: Dead heart in vegetative stage; white ear at reproductive stage.
Economic threshold: 5% dead hearts OR 2% white ears.
Control: Chlorpyrifos 20EC @ 1.25 L/ha at early infestation OR Cartap hydrochloride 50SP @ 1 kg/ha.
Pheromone traps: 5 traps/ha for monitoring.

Brown Plant Hopper (BPH):
Symptoms: Hopper burn — circular yellowing, then browning of plants.
Threshold: 10 hoppers per hill.
Control: Buprofezin 25SC @ 1 L/ha. Avoid Pyrethroids (cause BPH outbreaks).
Resistant varieties: Ratna, Sasyasree.

Blast (Pyricularia oryzae):
Neck blast at flowering is most damaging.
Control: Tricyclazole 75WP @ 500g/ha at booting + 10 days later.
Preventive spray when humidity > 90% and temperature 22–28°C.

Bacterial Leaf Blight (BLB):
Symptoms: Water-soaked margins, yellowing from tip, kresek in seedlings.
Control: No chemical cure. Use resistant varieties. Drain flooded fields. Spray Copper oxychloride 50WP @ 3g/L.
Preventive: Seed treatment with Streptomycin 500ppm + 1% lime water.

WEED MANAGEMENT
Major weeds: Echinochloa colonum (jungle rice), Cyperus rotundus (nutsedge), Monochoria.
Pretilachlor 50EC @ 1.5 L/ha at 3–5 DAS (puddled transplanted rice).
Bispyribac sodium 10SC @ 200 mL/ha at 15–20 DAT.
Manual weeding at 30 and 45 DAT if chemical not used.

HARVESTING
Harvest at 80–85% grain maturity (golden yellow).
Grain moisture at harvest: 20–25% (adjust combine settings).
Avoid delay — shattering loss and quality loss.
Paddy drying: Reduce to 14% moisture for 3-month storage, 12% for 6+ months.

YIELD
Transplanted irrigated: 50–60 quintals/ha
Direct seeded: 40–50 quintals/ha
Rainfed: 25–35 quintals/ha

MSP 2024-25: Rs. 2,369 per quintal
