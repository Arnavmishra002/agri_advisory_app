ICAR Package of Practices — Tomato, Potato, Onion (Vegetable Crops)
Source: ICAR-Indian Institute of Vegetable Research (IIVR), Varanasi; CPRI, Shimla

══════════════════════════════════════
TOMATO (Lycopersicon esculentum)
Season: Year-round in South; Rabi (Sep–Feb) in North.
══════════════════════════════════════

VARIETIES
Hybrid: Arka Samrat, Naveen, Pusa Hybrid-2, Syngenta Abhilash.
Open-pollinated: Pusa Ruby, Arka Vikas, CO-3.
Hybrid tomatoes yield 2–3× higher than OP varieties.

NURSERY AND TRANSPLANTING
Nursery: 100g seed/ha. Sow in raised beds 1m wide.
Seedling age: 25–30 days old, 5–7 leaf stage.
Spacing: 60×45 cm (irrigated field/hybrid).
Transplant in evening or cloudy weather. Water immediately.

FERTILIZER
N:P:K = 120:60:60 kg/ha.
Basal: Full P+K + 40 kg N.
Top dress at 30 DAS: 40 kg N.
Top dress at 60 DAS: 40 kg N.
Calcium: 25 kg/ha as calcium nitrate prevents Blossom End Rot.

MAJOR DISEASES

Early Blight (Alternaria solani):
Most common — circular dark spots with yellow halo, concentric rings.
Control: Mancozeb 75WP @ 2.5g/L spray every 7–10 days at disease onset.
Also: Azoxystrobin + Difenoconazole 18.3SC @ 1 mL/L.

Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans):
Water-soaked lesions in wet weather, white sporulation underneath.
Control: Metalaxyl+Mancozeb 72WP @ 2.5g/L every 5–7 days.
CRITICAL: Spray preventively during cloudy/rainy weather.

Tomato Leaf Curl Virus (ToLCV):
Transmitted by whitefly. Leaf curling, yellow mosaic.
No cure. Remove infected plants. Control whitefly with Imidacloprid 17.8SL @ 0.3 mL/L.

Fusarium Wilt:
Yellowing from lower leaves, vascular browning.
Trichoderma soil application @ 2.5 kg/ha before transplanting.

INSECT PESTS

Fruit Borer (Helicoverpa armigera):
Most serious — larvae tunnel into fruits.
ETL: 1 larva per plant.
Control: Emamectin benzoate 5SG @ 0.4g/L OR Spinosad 45SC @ 0.3 mL/L.
Pheromone traps: 10/ha.

Leaf Miner (Liriomyza spp.):
Serpentine mines in leaves.
Control: Abamectin 1.9EC @ 0.5 mL/L.

YIELD: 250–400 quintals/ha (hybrid); 150–200 q/ha (OP).

POTATO (Solanum tuberosum)
Season: Rabi (October–February). Major states: UP, WB, Bihar, Punjab, Gujarat.
══════════════════════════════════════

VARIETIES
Kufri Jyoti: Most popular. 90 days. Late blight tolerant.
Kufri Pukhraj: Early maturing 70 days. High yield.
Kufri Bahar: Long storage. Good for chips.
Kufri Chipsona-1/2/3: Processing/chips variety.
Atlantic: Specifically for processing industry.

SEED AND PLANTING
Seed rate: 20–25 quintals/ha (25–40g whole tubers or cut pieces).
Spacing: 60×20 cm (rows × plants) = 83,000 plants/ha.
Seed treatment: Mancozeb 75WP @ 3g/L dip for 15 minutes to prevent fungal diseases.
Planting depth: 5–7 cm.

FERTILIZER
N:P:K = 120–150:100:120 kg/ha.
Basal: Full P+K + 60 kg N. Apply in furrow.
Top dress: 60–90 kg N at earthing-up (30–35 DAS).
Potato is a heavy feeder — do not skip potassium, it improves tuber quality and starch.

MAJOR DISEASE — Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans):
Most serious disease of potato worldwide.
Symptoms: Water-soaked dark spots on leaves, white fungal growth at lesion border in humid conditions. Tuber infection causes brown rot.
SPRAY CALENDAR (preventive approach):
- First spray: 30–35 DAS (before disease appears).
- Subsequent sprays: Every 7–10 days during cool (10–20°C), humid weather.
Fungicides: Metalaxyl + Mancozeb 72WP @ 2.5g/L (systemic + contact).
Alternate with Cymoxanil + Mancozeb 72WP @ 2g/L to prevent resistance.
Stop spraying 10 days before harvest.

HARVESTING
Harvest when haulm (top) turns yellow.
Cut haulm 10 days before digging to harden skin.
Avoid harvesting in wet soil — increases bruising and rot.
Store in cool (4°C), dark, ventilated conditions.

YIELD: 200–300 quintals/ha.

ONION (Allium cepa)
Season: Kharif (May–Sept), Late Kharif (Jul–Nov), Rabi (Oct–May).
Major states: Maharashtra, Karnataka, MP, Bihar.
══════════════════════════════════════

VARIETIES
Rabi: Nasik Red (N-2-4-1), Agrifound Light Red, Bhima Kiran.
Kharif: Bhima Red, Agrifound Dark Red.
White onion: Bhima Shweta (for export).

TRANSPLANTING
Raise nursery for 6–8 weeks. Transplant 8–10 cm tall seedlings.
Spacing: 15×10 cm. Plant vertically, do not over-plant deep.
Irrigation immediately after transplanting.

FERTILIZER
N:P:K = 100:50:50 kg/ha.
Basal: Full P+K + 30 kg N.
Top dress: 35 kg N at 30 DAS + 35 kg N at 45 DAS.
Sulphur: 20–30 kg/ha improves pungency and storage life.

MAJOR DISEASES

Purple Blotch (Alternaria porri):
Purple lesions on leaves with water-soaked margin.
Control: Mancozeb 75WP + Iprodione (combo) @ 2g/L.

Stemphylium Blight:
Yellow to orange irregular spots. Leaf tip dieback.
Control: Chlorothalonil 75WP @ 2g/L.

Basal Rot (Fusarium):
Root and base rot, plant collapses.
Control: Trichoderma viride soil application @ 5 kg/ha.

THRIPS MANAGEMENT (Critical — vector for IYSV virus):
Thrips (Thrips tabaci): Most serious pest.
Control: Spinosad 45SC @ 0.3 mL/L OR Dimethoate 30EC @ 1 mL/L. Spray directed to leaf axils.
Monitor: Sticky blue traps 10–15/ha.

HARVESTING AND CURING
Harvest when 50–60% tops fall.
Cure in field for 3–5 days (sun drying).
Store in well-ventilated crates at cool temperature.
Rabi onion stores 4–6 months; Kharif onion stores 2–3 months only.

YIELD: 200–300 quintals/ha (Rabi); 150–200 q/ha (Kharif).
